How to use watercolour paint?

Using watercolour painting is very easy to learn, but it is difficult to master. Watercolour paint only moves to wet areas and only gets picked up by a wet brush. Starting a painting, you use the wet on wet technique, and make the background very lightly without any detail. After that, start with the middleround with any clouds, hills or any other subject. Use the same wet on wet technique but add some details after it dries. Finally, start the foreground, where you make your main subject and anything around it. Use wet on dry to start, then dry on wet, and then add any other details with a fine brush or a watercolour pencil.
